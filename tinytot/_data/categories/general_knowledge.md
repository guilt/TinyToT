## Chain 12: Macroeconomics and Global Economy
<!-- Handles: GDP, gross domestic product, recession, inflation causes, monetary policy, fiscal policy, comparative advantage, free trade, gini coefficient, human development, unemployment, business cycle, national debt, budget deficit, exchange rate, balance of payments, trade deficit, economic growth, market failure, public goods -->
Thought 1: Identify the macroeconomic concept — GDP, inflation mechanisms, fiscal policy, trade, inequality, or unemployment types?
Thought 2: GDP = C + I + G + (X−M). Two consecutive quarters of negative GDP growth = recession. Demand-pull inflation: excess consumer spending. Cost-push inflation: rising production costs. Fiscal policy: government spending and taxation to influence the economy. Comparative advantage: specialise in goods produced at relatively lower opportunity cost.
Thought 3: Frictional unemployment is normal job-searching. Structural unemployment is skills mismatch. Cyclical unemployment rises in recessions. The Gini coefficient (0–1) measures income inequality. Market failure: public goods (non-excludable), externalities (pollution), information asymmetry, monopoly power.
Conclusion: Macroeconomics: GDP (C+I+G+(X-M)) measures total output; two negative quarters = recession. Inflation types: demand-pull (excess spending), cost-push (production costs), monetary (excess money supply). Fiscal policy: expansionary (spend more / tax less) vs contractionary. Comparative advantage (Ricardo): countries specialise where they have relatively lower opportunity cost. Types of unemployment: frictional (between jobs), structural (skills mismatch), cyclical (recession-driven). Gini coefficient: 0 = perfect equality, 1 = maximum inequality. Market failure arises from public goods, externalities, and information asymmetry.

Conclusion: Law essentials: Criminal law — actus reus (guilty act) + mens rea (guilty intent) required; standard of proof: beyond reasonable doubt.
