# Science and Vocabulary

## Atomic Numbers and Chemistry

The atomic number of hydrogen is 1. The atomic number of helium is 2.
The atomic number of carbon is 6. Carbon is the basis of all organic chemistry.
The atomic number of nitrogen is 7. The atomic number of oxygen is 8.
The atomic number of sodium is 11 (symbol Na). The atomic number of iron is 26 (symbol Fe).
The atomic number of gold is 79 (symbol Au). The atomic number of silver is 47 (symbol Ag).
The periodic table has 118 confirmed elements.

## Cell Biology

The mitochondria is the powerhouse of the cell. It produces ATP through cellular respiration.
The nucleus contains the cell's DNA and controls cell activities.
The cell membrane controls what enters and exits the cell.
Ribosomes are responsible for protein synthesis.
The endoplasmic reticulum is involved in protein and lipid synthesis.
Chloroplasts are found in plant cells and perform photosynthesis.

## Photosynthesis and Respiration

Plants absorb carbon dioxide (CO2) during photosynthesis. They also absorb water and sunlight.
Photosynthesis produces glucose and oxygen. The equation is: CO2 + H2O + light → glucose + O2.
Cellular respiration is the reverse: glucose + O2 → CO2 + H2O + energy (ATP).
Plants release oxygen as a byproduct of photosynthesis.

## Human Body

The adult human body has 206 bones. Babies are born with approximately 270 bones that fuse over time.
The human brain weighs approximately 1.4 kg (3 pounds).
The human heart has four chambers: two atria and two ventricles.
The largest organ in the human body is the skin.
The human body has approximately 37 trillion cells.
Blood type ABO system has four types: A, B, AB, and O.
The normal body temperature is 37 degrees Celsius (98.6 degrees Fahrenheit).

## Physics

Newton's first law: an object in motion stays in motion unless acted upon by an external force.
Newton's second law: Force = mass × acceleration (F = ma).
Newton's third law: for every action there is an equal and opposite reaction.
The speed of sound in air at sea level is approximately 343 metres per second.
The speed of light in a vacuum is 299,792,458 metres per second.
E = mc² is Einstein's mass-energy equivalence formula (E is energy, m is mass, c is speed of light).

## Geography

The longest river in the world is the Nile River in Africa. Some measurements put the Amazon second.
The Amazon River in South America has the largest discharge by volume.
The largest continent is Asia. The smallest continent is Australia (or Oceania).
The largest ocean is the Pacific Ocean. The smallest ocean is the Arctic Ocean.
Egypt is located in northeastern Africa and the Sinai Peninsula.
The capital of Japan is Tokyo. The capital of China is Beijing. The capital of India is New Delhi.
The capital of Brazil is Brasilia. The capital of Australia is Canberra.
The capital of Canada is Ottawa. The capital of Mexico is Mexico City.
The capital of Russia is Moscow. The capital of South Africa has three capitals: Pretoria, Cape Town, and Bloemfontein.
The Sahara is the largest hot desert in the world, located in North Africa.
Mount Everest at 8,848.86 metres is the highest mountain above sea level.
The Dead Sea is the lowest point on Earth's surface at about 430 metres below sea level.

## History

World War I began in 1914 when Archduke Franz Ferdinand was assassinated. It ended in 1918.
World War II began in 1939 when Germany invaded Poland. It ended in 1945.
The Berlin Wall was built in 1961 and fell on November 9, 1989.
George Washington was the first president of the United States, serving from 1789 to 1797.
The Declaration of Independence was signed in 1776.
The French Revolution began in 1789.
The Moon landing occurred on July 20, 1969, when Apollo 11 astronauts Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin walked on the Moon.
The internet was invented in the late 1960s as ARPANET. The World Wide Web was invented by Tim Berners-Lee in 1989.

## Vocabulary and Language

A synonym for happy is joyful, content, pleased, glad, or elated.
A synonym for sad is unhappy, sorrowful, melancholy, or dejected.
A synonym for big is large, huge, enormous, or gigantic.
A synonym for smart is intelligent, clever, brilliant, or astute.
The plural of mouse is mice. The plural of goose is geese. The plural of child is children.
The plural of tooth is teeth. The plural of foot is feet. The plural of person is people.
Ubiquitous means present, appearing, or found everywhere. It is synonymous with omnipresent.
Ephemeral means lasting for a very short time. It is synonymous with transient or fleeting.
Verbose means using more words than needed. It is synonymous with wordy or long-winded.
Ambiguous means open to more than one interpretation. It is synonymous with unclear or vague.
An antonym is a word with the opposite meaning. Antonyms of hot include cold, cool, and frigid.

## Logic and Mathematics

In logic, if P implies Q and P is true, then Q is true (modus ponens).
In logic, if P implies Q and Q is false, then P is false (modus tollens).
The transitive property: if A > B and B > C, then A > C. This applies to any transitive relation.
No fish can walk because fish are aquatic vertebrates without legs adapted for walking.
All mammals are warm-blooded. All dogs are mammals. Therefore all dogs are warm-blooded.
Socrates is mortal because all humans are mortal and Socrates is human.
